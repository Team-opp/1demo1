🏥 RadTemplates — Peer Review Scoring

⭐ Peer Review Scoring — 0 to 4

Every reviewed report receives a score from 0 to 4. Lower is better. Here is what each score means:

0
No Error
Report is complete and accurate. No discrepancy found. Case closes automatically.
1
Minor
Small omission or wording issue with no clinical impact. Can close with simple agreement.
2
Moderate
Missed finding with potential clinical significance. Written response required.
3
Significant
Clear error that could affect patient management. Full written response required.
4
Critical
Major diagnostic error with serious patient safety implications. Mandatory escalation.
💡 Key rule: Score 0–1 can close with a simple agreement. Score 2 or above requires your written explanation. If you believe the reviewer's assessment is wrong, escalate to Adjudication for an HR binding decision.
